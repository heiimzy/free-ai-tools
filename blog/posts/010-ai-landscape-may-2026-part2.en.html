AI Landscape May 2026: Record Funding, Agentic AI Regulation, Apple Opens Siri to Third Parties

May 2026 is shaping up as a historic month for AI — not just for model releases, but for the business and regulatory infrastructure being built around them. OpenAI closed the largest private financing in history. Anthropic signed partnerships with Google, SpaceX, and Wall Street. The White House moved toward pre-release AI safety reviews. And in a move that would have been unthinkable two years ago, Apple announced it will let users choose third-party AI models as their default assistant. Here's your complete briefing.

1. The Mega-Deals Redefining AI Finance

OpenAI's $122 Billion Round: The Largest Private Fundraising Ever

OpenAI closed a $122 billion funding round at an $852 billion post-money valuation — the largest private financing in history, backed by SoftBank, Microsoft, and other major investors. The company disclosed that it expects to spend $50 billion on compute in 2026 alone, underscoring just how capital-intensive the frontier AI business really is.

In a related move, OpenAI launched a beta of ChatGPT Ads Manager, signaling a shift toward an ad-supported revenue model alongside its subscription business. The company also discussed spinning out its robotics and consumer hardware divisions, though the plan was rejected internally.

Perhaps most notably, OpenAI formed a $10 billion joint venture called "The Deployment Company" with TPG and Bain Capital, specifically tasked with helping large enterprises implement AI systems — a recognition that API access alone isn't enough for enterprise adoption.

Anthropic's Trillion-Dollar Trajectory

Anthropic's growth has been nothing short of astonishing. The company's annualized revenue has surged from roughly $9 billion at the end of 2025 to over $44 billion — an 80x increase against internal projections. At its peak, Anthropic's implied valuation touched $1 trillion.

The catalysts:

  • $40 billion investment agreement with Google (with up to $40B total), announced at Google's Cloud Next 2026 event
  • SpaceX Colossus partnership: access to 220,000+ NVIDIA GPUs at the Memphis Colossus 1 facility, plus joint exploration of orbital AI data centers
  • $1.8 billion Akamai CDN deal — the largest in Akamai's history, handling Anthropic's massive inference traffic
  • $1.5 billion enterprise JV with Goldman Sachs, Blackstone, and Hellman & Friedman to deploy Claude inside mid-sized companies

Anthropic's Mythos Preview also became the first AI model to pass the UK AI Security Institute's 32-step offensive cyber test range, crossing what experts are calling a "cyber threshold." CEO Dario Amodei called the moment a "moment of danger," warning about autonomous cyber capabilities.

Cohere + Aleph Alpha: European Sovereign AI Alliance

Canadian AI company Cohere merged with Germany's Aleph Alpha to form a joint "sovereign AI" entity, creating a European AI champion capable of competing with US and Chinese players. The merger reflects growing demand for AI systems built on European infrastructure and subject to European data regulations.

2. Apple's iOS 27: The Walled Garden Opens to AI

In a historic shift, Apple announced that iOS 27 will allow users to choose third-party AI models — including Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, and others — as alternatives to Siri. This is the first time Apple has opened its core AI assistant to external providers.

The implications are enormous: iPhone users will soon be able to pick their preferred AI assistant, breaking Siri's default monopoly. For AI companies, it provides direct access to over 1 billion active iPhone users. For Apple, it's a pragmatic admission that no single company can offer the best AI for every use case.

Industry analysts predict this will spark a wave of competition among AI providers to offer the best on-device experience, with features like low latency, privacy preservation, and deep integration with iOS APIs becoming key differentiators.

3. AI Regulation: The Era of Pre-Release Safety Reviews Begins

White House Considers Executive Order for Frontier AI Reviews

The White House is considering an executive order that would require pre-release safety reviews for frontier AI models, similar to the FDA's drug approval process. The order would mandate that companies demonstrate their models meet safety benchmarks before public deployment.

Microsoft, Google, and xAI have already agreed to provide the US government with early access to their AI models for national security testing. This voluntary framework could become mandatory under the new executive order.

CISA Issues First Federal Guidance for Agentic AI

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) released its first-ever federal guidance on the "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI", urging agencies to apply zero-trust principles when integrating AI agents into government systems. A Market Connections survey found that 53% of federal agencies are now planning agentic AI pilot programs.

The guidance covers four key areas: access control for AI agents, continuous monitoring of agent behavior, human-in-the-loop requirements for high-impact decisions, and data provenance tracking.

EU Blocks Solar Funding Over Huawei AI Cyber Concerns

The European Union blocked funding for solar energy projects using Huawei inverters, citing cybersecurity risks related to potential AI-driven grid attacks. The decision highlights how AI security concerns are increasingly influencing broader technology policy decisions.

4. Enterprise AI: From Experiments to Production

74% of Fortune 500 Companies Have Deployed Autonomous Agents

A new industry survey revealed that 74% of Fortune 500 companies have deployed at least one autonomous AI agent in production. The most common use cases are customer service automation, internal knowledge management, and software development assistance.

The CMS reported $26.3 billion in savings from AI-powered fraud detection systems, delivering a $14.60 ROI per dollar spent. These real-world numbers are driving further investment despite ongoing concerns about reliability and safety.

The CPU Bottleneck: Agentic AI's Hidden Performance Wall

New research reveals a surprising bottleneck in AI agent performance: CPUs account for 40-90% of latency in AI agent tasks. While GPUs handle inference efficiently, the orchestration logic, tool calls, and context management that agents perform all run on CPUs — and current server CPUs weren't designed for this workload.

The market has responded: CPU prices are up 10-15%, with lead times extending to 8-12 weeks. This is creating a secondary hardware crunch that compounds the well-known GPU shortage. AI infrastructure planners should factor CPU capacity into their scaling projections — it may become the limiting factor sooner than expected.

5. Hardware & Infrastructure: AMD Shines, Energy Innovation

AMD Data Center Revenue Hits $5.6B

AMD reported data center revenue of $5.6 billion in Q1 2026, up 52% year-over-year, driven by its MI300 series AI accelerators. The overall AI compute market is projected to reach $1.2 trillion in 2026.

The scale of AI infrastructure demand is creating unusual side effects: Denmark paused new grid connections after data center applications reached 60 GW of requested capacity — versus the country's 7 GW peak demand. Energy availability is becoming a bottleneck for AI expansion.

Liquid AI Models: 60% Less Cooling

A new generation of "liquid AI" models demonstrated 60% less cooling energy compared to 2024-era architectures. This represents meaningful progress in making AI infrastructure more sustainable, though absolute energy demand continues to grow due to rapid deployment scaling.

6. Breakthrough Research: π0.7 Robotics Model, Agent-World

Two research milestones stood out this month:

  • π0.7 (Pi Zero 0.7): The first robotics foundation model demonstrating zero-shot transfer across unseen tasks and robot embodiments — meaning a robot trained in simulation could adapt to a completely different physical robot without retraining.
  • Agent-World: An environment synthesis technique achieving state-of-the-art results with 8B and 14B parameter models, matching the performance of 685B-parameter models. This could dramatically reduce the compute requirements for training capable AI agents.

However, the ClawBench benchmark showed that even the best AI agent scored only 33.3% on real-world web tasks — a reminder that while progress is rapid, we're still far from human-level agentic capability.

7. Notable Industry Moves

  • Skild AI acquired Zebra Technologies' robotics division (Fetch Robotics + Symmetry) for an undisclosed sum
  • SpaceX placed a $60B buyout option on AI coding startup Cursor (pre-IPO)
  • China blocked Meta's proposed $2B acquisition of AI agent startup Manus
  • Ineffable Intelligence closed a $1.1B seed round — the largest in European history for a seed-stage startup
  • $60 trillion asset manager Fidelity and 18 other global asset managers made a "multi-generational" commitment to AI compute infrastructure

The bottom line: May 2026 is the month AI went from being a technology story to being a business and regulatory story. The amounts of capital involved ($122B rounds, trillion-dollar valuations), the scale of infrastructure (orbital data centers, gigawatt power requirements), and the speed of regulatory response (White House executive orders, CISA guidance, Apple opening its ecosystem) all signal that AI has permanently crossed into the mainstream of global economic and political life.
