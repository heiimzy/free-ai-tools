Enterprise AI ROI in 2026: 8 Real Case Studies With Measurable Results

In May 2026, Conduent revealed that its AI-powered FastCap platform identified $18 million in savings for a single client over six months. Home Depot expanded its AI voice agent pilot to 50 stores after early tests showed call resolution 4x faster than traditional phone menus. Georgia-Pacific reported saving $1 million per machine with predictive maintenance on AWS.

These aren't pilot projects or proof-of-concepts. Enterprise AI has entered the era of measurable ROI. Here are eight real case studies from May 2026, organized by business function, each with hard numbers.

Manufacturing: Predictive Maintenance & Quality Inspection

Georgia-Pacific

Predictive Maintenance: $1M Saved Per Machine Per Year

The paper products giant deployed machine learning models on AWS for condition-based monitoring. The system can predict equipment failure 60-90 days in advance. One conversion line eliminated 40% of parent roll tears — a long-standing multimillion-dollar problem. Operators interact with a GenAI chatbot (GP Chat, built on Amazon Bedrock) that combines IoT sensor data with natural language queries. Estimated savings: $1 million per machine annually.

General Manufacturing (Industry Data)

The 80% Failure Rule — and Why It Still Pays Off

Major manufacturers report that approximately 80% of AI pilot projects fail. The ones that succeed, however, deliver outsized returns. The key differentiator isn't technology — it's organizational readiness. Companies that embed engineers on production lines (sometimes for months) before deploying AI see dramatically higher success rates. The message: AI deployment is 70% about changing how people work, 30% about technology.

Finance & Procurement: The Highest Clarity ROI

Conduent FastCap

$18M Identified in 6 Months With GenAI + Agentic AI

Conduent's FastCap platform combines generative AI with agentic AI for financial analysis. For one global logistics client over six months: supplier optimization found $6M in savings, contract compliance audits caught $2M in overpayments, and the system detected $7M in duplicate charges that had been missed by existing systems. Total: $15M directly identified. For an automotive manufacturer, FastCap processed 43,000 bid requests in 4 months, identifying over $3M in additional savings.

Rebel Cheese (SMB Case)

Small Company, AI-Level Auditing: $400K Saved

Mark Cuban-backed vegan cheese company (valued at ~$20M) discovered a carrier was overcharging them by ~$250K after a holiday season. They built an AI agent that audits invoices line-by-line against contracts and detects box bulge (to 1/8 inch precision) in shipping photos. Result: $400,000 saved in one year — ~$40K per month. Founder: "Even two full-time hires couldn't catch all the discrepancies. The data volume is too large. This is what AI is actually good at." This case demonstrates how AI is leveling the competitive playing field between small businesses and enterprises.

Home Depot

Voice AI Agents: 50-Store Pilot, 4x Faster Resolution

In May 2026, Home Depot expanded its AI voice agent pilot to 50 stores. The system identifies caller intent within 10 seconds and resolves calls 4x faster than traditional IVR phone menus. Key insight: the voice agent doesn't just route calls — it completes transactions. A customer calling about a tool rental can check availability, reserve, and get a confirmation without speaking to a human. Nationwide rollout is planned based on pilot results.

Verint Quality Bot

Automated Quality Assurance: $12.5M/Year Saved

Verint's Quality Bot automatically reviews 97% of all call recordings — a scale impossible for human QA teams. Annual savings: $12.5 million per client, with supervisor capacity improved by 80%. The bot doesn't just monitor — it identifies coaching opportunities, compliance risks, and sentiment trends across millions of interactions.

Translation & Knowledge Management

Wordly

AI Translation: $200M+ in Cumulative Customer Savings

Wordly's real-time AI translation platform has generated over $200 million in customer savings since 2019, with customers saving 50%+ versus conventional live translation. Public sector usage grew 5x over two years. In May 2026, Wordly launched an ROI calculator to help organizations compare traditional vs AI translation costs — a sign that the market has matured to demand transparent ROI metrics.

eGain's AI Knowledge Hub reported 26% YoY ARR growth to $48M, now representing 64% of total SaaS recurring revenue. Net income surged 3,486% (from $66K to $2.4M) with gross margins expanding from 68% to 73%.

What Separates Winners From the Rest?

These eight cases reveal clear patterns:

1. Start with transparent cost structures. Finance audit, translation replacement, and customer service automation all share one trait: the cost baseline is known. You can calculate ROI before writing a line of code. Manufacturing AI is harder to measure but the payoffs are larger when you get it right.

2. Data sovereignty is the strongest ROI predictor. An MIT Technology Review study (May 2026) found that organizations with the strongest data sovereignty commitment report 5x the AI ROI of peers. The correlation coefficient is 0.93 — the single strongest factor identified. 95% of organizations plan to establish their own AI and data platforms within 3 years.

3. Organizational change is the bottleneck. The 80% failure rate on AI pilots isn't about technology quality. It's about whether the organization is ready to change how people work. Companies that invest in change management — embedding engineers on production lines, retraining affected workers, redesigning workflows — see dramatically different outcomes.

4. Small businesses now have enterprise-grade AI tools. The Rebel Cheese case is emblematic: a $20M company built AI auditing capability that would have required a Fortune 500 budget five years ago. The barrier to entry for AI-powered efficiency is lower than it has ever been.

The bottom line: Enterprise AI ROI in 2026 is no longer a question of "if" but "where" and "how." Choose a transparent-cost use case, fix the organizational readiness problem first, and let the data speak. The technology is the easy part.
