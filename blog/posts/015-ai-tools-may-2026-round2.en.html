AI Tools Roundup May 2026: General Compute ASIC Cloud, Claude Goes Big on SMB & Legal, Boomi Agent Governance

The week of May 12-16, 2026 saw one of the most intense bursts of AI product launches this year. From purpose-built inference hardware for AI agents to enterprise governance frameworks and vertical industry tools, the pattern is clear: the AI tool market is rapidly maturing from "experiment with models" to "deploy with platforms." Here's everything you need to know about the week's biggest releases.

1. General Compute: The First ASIC-First Inference Cloud for AI Agents

Launch date: May 15, 2026

San Francisco-based General Compute Inc. exited stealth with a product that may redefine how AI agents consume compute. Their platform is the first purpose-built inference cloud running on custom ASICs — not repurposed GPUs — designed from the ground up for AI agent workloads.

The key architectural innovation is the separation of prefill and decode stages, which allows each phase to scale independently. For agent workloads — which typically involve long context prefill followed by short, bursty decoding — this means dramatically better resource utilization than general-purpose GPU clouds.

Other notable features include an API optimized for both humans AND AI agents (agents can programmatically provision their own compute), hydroelectric-powered data centers with air-cooled hardware, and support for a range of open-source LLMs at launch.

Why it matters: As AI agents move from demo to production, the compute layer is becoming a bottleneck. General Compute's ASIC-first approach addresses an emerging pain point that GPU-centric clouds weren't designed for. If agents are the future of AI, they need infrastructure built for them — not retrofitted from training infrastructure.

2. Boomi Enterprise AI Agent Governance Platform

Announced: May 14, 2026

Boomi — one of the leading integration platform as a service (iPaaS) providers — released a major expansion of its AI capabilities with a focus on enterprise agent governance. The update includes five major new capabilities:

  • Governed Agent Connectivity — over 1,000 MCP-enabled tool connectors with built-in policy enforcement
  • AI Gateway — centralized policy controls for rate limiting, content filtering, and audit logging
  • Orchestrated Agentic Workflows — natural-language workflow builder that composes multi-step, multi-agent processes
  • Agent SIM (labs preview) — a simulation environment to test agent behavior before production deployment
  • Distributed Agent Runtime — on-premises deployment option for regulated industries

Why it matters: Enterprise adoption of AI agents has been stalling precisely because of governance concerns — CIOs don't want autonomous code running inside their infrastructure without guardrails. Boomi's governance-first approach addresses this head-on. CISA's recent federal guidance on agentic AI (released May 2026) explicitly recommends zero-trust architectures for agent deployments, making products like this increasingly essential for compliance.

3. Anthropic's Two-Pronged Vertical Push: Small Business & Legal

Claude for Small Business (May 13)

Anthropic released a complete automation suite targeting SMBs, with deep integrations into the tools small businesses actually use: QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, and DocuSign. The suite covers cash flow forecasting, automated collections, month-end close, ad campaign generation, CRM management, and contract review — all through a natural-language interface.

To overcome the adoption barrier that plagues SMB software, Anthropic also launched free on-demand AI training courses and in-person workshops in a 10-city tour starting in Chicago. The message is: we're not just selling software, we're teaching you how to use it.

Claude for Legal (May 12)

Anthropic's legal industry push is equally aggressive. The company released 20+ new integrations for legal workflows, including 12 role-specific AI plugins (M&A due diligence, employment handbook drafting, litigation document review, etc.) and cross-app Microsoft 365 integration. The underlying model — Claude Opus 4.7 — scored 90.9% on Harvey's BigLaw Bench.

Major law firms are already on board: Freshfields, Quinn Emanuel, Holland & Knight, and Crosby Legal have all adopted the platform. Fortune called it "Big Law going all in on AI."

Why it matters: Vertical AI products — purpose-built for specific industries rather than general-purpose chatbots — represent the next wave of AI tool adoption. Anthropic's simultaneous push into SMB and Legal shows that the company is betting on depth over breadth: owning specific workflows entirely rather than being a generic assistant.

4. Mixpanel AI: Always-On Product Intelligence

Announced: May 12, 2026

Mixpanel — the product analytics platform — launched Mixpanel AI, an always-on product intelligence system that runs six specialized sub-agents:

  • Onboarding Agent — guides new users through product analytics setup
  • Dashboard Agent — auto-generates and maintains analytics dashboards
  • Experiment Agent — designs, runs, and analyzes A/B tests
  • Root Cause Analysis Agent — investigates metric anomalies autonomously
  • KPI Monitoring Agent — continuously tracks key metrics and alerts on changes
  • Slack-native integration — operates directly within Slack conversations

Mixpanel AI also integrates natively with Claude, ChatGPT, and Cursor — allowing product teams to query analytics data from within their AI coding environment.

5. Liminary AI Research Agents

Launched on Product Hunt: May 13, 2026

Liminary launched two products aimed at enterprise knowledge workers: AI Research Agents (custom recurring research agents that monitor topics and deliver briefings) and Meeting Companion (live recall that connects past context from emails, chats, and docs to current meetings).

Their benchmark results are striking: 88.9% accuracy on factual QA against personal data, compared to 63% for ChatGPT 5.4 + Google Drive. The difference comes from a persistent memory layer that maintains context across sessions — something most AI tools still struggle with.

6. Coming Next Week: Google I/O 2026 Preview

May 19, 2026 — Google I/O promises to be the biggest developer event of the year. Leaks and reports suggest three major reveals:

  • Gemini 4 — a leapfrog model release designed to reclaim the frontier benchmark crown
  • Android XR smart glasses — built in partnership with Warby Parker, marking Google's return to wearable AI
  • Aluminium OS — a unified operating system merging Android and Chrome OS, with Gemini deeply embedded at the OS level

For AI tool watchers, I/O week will be the most important event of May — potentially reshaping the competitive landscape across mobile, wearables, and desktop AI integration.

The Big Picture

Looking across this week's launches, three themes stand out:

Infrastructure gets Agent-Native. General Compute's ASIC cloud is the first infrastructure play explicitly designed for the agent era. Expect more to follow.

Vertical AI is where the value is. Anthropic's SMB and Legal pushes, Mixpanel's AI agents, and Boomi's governance tools all point in the same direction: the money in AI tools isn't in general-purpose chatbots — it's in deeply integrated, industry-specific solutions.

Enterprise governance is no longer optional. With Boomi adding governance layers, CISA issuing federal guidance, and the White House considering executive orders on AI safety, any company deploying AI agents without proper governance is taking a serious risk. The "move fast and break things" era of AI is over — the "governed deployment" era has begun.
