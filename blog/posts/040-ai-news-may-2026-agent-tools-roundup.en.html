Today's AI News: Anthropic CEO Predicts Free Software, Tencent Enters AI Design, and the Agent-Native Movement Accelerates

1. Anthropic CEO: Claude Features Are Now Almost Entirely AI-Developed, Software Heading Toward Free

Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic, gave a wide-ranging interview today revealing that Claude's new features are now "almost entirely" built by AI systems themselves — including Claude Code and other internal development agents. The claim, if accurate, represents a significant milestone in AI self-improvement: the product and the tool used to build it are converging.

Even more striking was Amodei's prediction about the future of software: "Software will head toward being free." His reasoning is that as AI development capabilities improve, the marginal cost of building and maintaining software approaches zero. This doesn't mean all software companies disappear, but it suggests a fundamental shift in how value is captured — moving from selling software licenses to selling access, compute, or outcomes.

The interview follows a pattern of AI lab executives making increasingly bold predictions. But Amodei's claim is notable because it comes from the CEO of a company whose main product is an AI model — giving him a direct line of sight into how fast AI coding capabilities are accelerating. If Claude's own features are being built by Claude itself, the feedback loop is already in motion.

Why "AI-Developed AI" Matters

The concept of AI systems building their own successors has been discussed theoretically for years under the rubric of "recursive self-improvement." Amodei's interview is one of the first times a major AI lab CEO has stated publicly that this is already happening internally. The implications for development speed are profound: if the bottleneck is no longer human developer availability but compute resources and evaluation quality, the pace of AI capability gains could shift dramatically. It also raises questions about the future role of human software engineers — not obsolescence, but a shift toward oversight, architecture, and defining what to build rather than how to build it.

2. Tencent Launches Ardot: An AI-Native Design Collaboration Platform

Tencent officially launched Ardot today, an AI-powered UI/UX design collaboration platform now in public beta. Ardot supports real-time multi-person collaboration and covers the full software design workflow — from visual design to code delivery, team collaboration, and asset management.

The key differentiator is natural language-driven design: users describe interface requirements in plain language, and Ardot streams editable design drafts in real-time. It also offers one-click code export and direct Figma file import with full preservation of layouts, styles, and components — effectively zero-cost migration from Figma.

Ardot positions itself against established players like Figma and Sketch, but with an AI-native architecture that treats AI collaboration as the default rather than an add-on. For the Chinese market, where Figma access has been inconsistent due to regulatory issues, Ardot could capture significant mindshare among product teams looking for an integrated AI design workflow.

3. CLI-Anything: 35.9k Stars for Making All Software Agent-Native

CLI-Anything, a project from HKUDS (University of Hong Kong Data Science lab), has skyrocketed to 35,910 GitHub stars with a bold mission: making every software application agent-native. The project provides CLI harnesses and agent integration layers for dozens of applications — from Blender and Obsidian to GIMP and LibreOffice — allowing AI agents to control them programmatically.

The project's scope is staggering. It includes agent harnesses for 50+ applications across categories including 3D modeling (Blender), audio editing (Audacity), note-taking (Obsidian), diagramming (Draw.io), browser automation, database management, and even game engines (Godot). Each harness translates natural language instructions into application-specific commands, effectively giving AI agents "hands" to use existing software.

The rapid star growth reflects a broader industry recognition: the next frontier for AI agents isn't building new tools — it's making existing tools accessible to agents. CLI-Anything's approach of lightweight CLI wrappers rather than deep API integrations is pragmatic and scales well.

4. Alibaba Qwen Teases "Heavyweight New Friend" Ahead of May 20 Summit

Alibaba's Qwen (Tongyi Qianwen) large model team teased an announcement today: a "heavyweight new friend" will appear at the Alibaba Cloud Summit on May 20. The official teaser keywords include: more capable, more powerful, deep, and broad.

While the company is keeping details under wraps, speculation centers on either a new flagship Qwen model (potentially Qwen 3.5 or Qwen 4 with significantly improved reasoning) or a major product launch that pairs Qwen with a new Alibaba Cloud service. The timing — just two days away — means we won't have to wait long to find out.

Alibaba has been steadily investing in its AI ecosystem, and the Qwen series has become one of the most widely adopted open-weight model families in China. Any significant upgrade would ripple through the domestic AI application landscape.

5. Semble: Code Search for AI Agents That Uses 98% Fewer Tokens

Semble, a semantic code search tool designed specifically for AI coding agents, hit the Hacker News front page with 248 points. Its value proposition is straightforward and compelling: it reduces token consumption for code search by 98% compared to traditional grep-based approaches, while returning more relevant results.

For AI agents that need to understand large codebases, the savings are transformative. A typical search that might cost $0.10 in token fees drops to $0.002. More importantly, the freed context window capacity can be redirected to actual reasoning and code generation — making the agent more effective on complex tasks.

Semble understands code semantics rather than just matching text patterns, returning only the code fragments an agent actually needs. For developers building agentic coding workflows, this is the kind of infrastructure improvement that compounds across every session.

6. OpenHuman: The Open-Source Personal AI Assistant Trending 14k Stars

OpenHuman, an open-source project positioning itself as "your personal AI super intelligence," has gathered 14,144 GitHub stars today. The project offers a desktop application with a unique approach: an AI assistant with a visible desktop mascot that speaks, reacts to surroundings, joins video calls as a participant, and remembers context across weeks.

Key features include 118+ third-party integrations, cross-platform support (macOS, Windows, Linux), and a UI-first design philosophy that prioritizes human interaction over configuration. The project is in early beta but has already attracted significant community interest, suggesting strong demand for open-source alternatives to proprietary AI assistants like Claude and ChatGPT that offer persistent memory and proactive behavior.

The elephant in the room: OpenHuman's architecture bears similarities to projects like Claude Code and OpenAI's Codex agent, but repackaged as a general-purpose personal assistant rather than a coding tool. If the project delivers on its roadmap, it could become a compelling open-source alternative for users who want an AI assistant that lives on their desktop, knows their habits, and acts autonomously.

Trend of the Day: The Agent-Native Infrastructure Layer

Looking across today's stories, a clear pattern emerges: the AI industry is building the infrastructure layer for agent-native software. CLI-Anything makes existing applications controllable by agents. Semble optimizes how agents search code. Tencent Ardot represents a new generation of AI-native creative tools. Anthropic's CEO reveals that Claude builds itself. And OpenHuman packages all of this into a consumer-facing personal AI.

Each of these stories, in isolation, looks like a product launch or a research milestone. Together, they paint a picture of an industry that has moved past the "ChatGPT as chat interface" phase and is now systematically building the plumbing, tools, and interfaces for a world where AI agents are first-class citizens of the software ecosystem.
