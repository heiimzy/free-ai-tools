1. AI Policy Risks: Wall Street Legend Warns of Balance Sheet Reduction as Biggest "Achilles Heel"

Victor Sperandeo: Investors Focus on Wrong Policy Lever

Legendary Wall Street trader Victor Sperandeo has pointed out that investors in the current AI-fueled US stock market are overly focused on whether the Federal Reserve will cut the federal funds rate, while neglecting changes in the Fed's balance sheet. His point is simple: rate cuts can lower the cost of capital, but don't necessarily make capital more accessible.

Sperandeo, who achieved investment profits for 12 consecutive years from 1978 to 1989 and was nicknamed "Trader Vic" by Wall Street, believes that while rate cuts are an interest rate lever, balance sheet reduction is the real "achilles heel" — it directly impacts market liquidity, which is key to whether AI company valuations can be sustained.

Liquidity Concerns Behind the AI Frenzy

Current AI company valuations are generally high, largely dependent on ample market liquidity. If the Fed continues to reduce its balance sheet and market liquidity tightens, AI companies' financing environment and valuation logic will face challenges. This is why Sperandeo believes investors should pay more attention to the Fed's balance sheet policy.
